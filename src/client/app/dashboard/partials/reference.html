So You Want to Write an Article?

This guide will go over the basics of using StoryBored's editor, and much more!

The Basics of Markdown Editing

Our editor uses the Markdown language to convert your content into valid HTML to display in browsers without forcing you to learn or use HTML. For a full cheatsheet go here.

Headers

There are six levels of headers available in Markdown. To denote a header, 1-6 hash characters are used at the start of a line.

Input:

# level 1
## level 2
### level 3
#### level 4
##### level 5
###### level 6

Output:

Level 1

Level 2

Level 3

Level 4

Level 5
Level 6

Blockquotes

Blockquotes are denoted at the beginning of a line via the ">" symbol.

Input:

> Big Bada Boom -Bruce Willis

Output:

Big Bada Boom -Bruce Willis

Lists

Markdown has ordered lists and unordered lists. To denote an ordered list use the number of the item you are on and a period like so:

Input:

1. one
2. two
3. three

Output:

  1. one
  2. two
  3. three

An unordered list can be denoted using your choice of asterisks, pluses, and hyphens at the beginning of a line.

Input:

* one
* two
* three

Output:

  • one
  • two
  • three

Horizontal Rules

If you feel like being fancy and adding a line to separate parts of your article, horizontal rules can be added by placing three ore more hyphens, asterisks, or underscores on a line by themselves.

Input:

text
---
more text

Output:

text

more text

Putting Images on The Web in Your Articles

If the image you want to add to your article already exists on the web, you can put it in your article in the following ways:
Note that alt text is the text someone with a disability would use to identify an image.

Inline Style

Input:

![alt text](http://storybored.news/img/profiles/7f32861d-1177-4458-954f-d844d7cd3576.jpg)

Output:

HTML Style

For more fine-grained control of the way your image is presented you may want to use this way of including your images.

Input:

<img src="http://storybored.news/img/profiles/7f32861d-1177-4458-954f-d844d7cd3576.jpg" alt="alt text"/>

Output:

Scale image based on the width of the article:

Input:

<img class="img-responsive" src="http://storybored.news/img/profiles/7f32861d-1177-4458-954f-d844d7cd3576.jpg" alt="alt text"/>

Output:

Centering an image within your article:

Input:

<img class="img-responsive center-block" src="http://storybored.news/img/profiles/7f32861d-1177-4458-954f-d844d7cd3576.jpg" alt="alt text"/>

Putting Images on the Web With Cloudinary

In the Upload Images tab on the dashboard, users have the ability to upload images from their devices to Cloudinary, a cloud-based content distribution network. Once uploaded, users can access their images via a public url. In our system, when an image is uploaded, the column titled Markdown Image is populated with a copy/pastable link ready for use within articles.
